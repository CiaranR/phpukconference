Diversity

PHP UK Conference is dedicated to improving diversity in the technology industry. Over the past few years, we've taken steps towards making PHP UK a welcoming and inclusive event for all attendees. Some of the changes we've implemented are:

  • 2014 - Created a Code of Conduct for all PHP London events
  • 2015 - Revised Call for Papers process to include anonymised voting

PHP UK Conference Scholarship Programme

PHP UK's scholarship program provides aide to members of underrepresented groups who may not otherwise be able to attend our conference for financial reasons.

Eligibility

Applicants should be a member of an underrepresented group in tech - women, minorities, LGBTQIA, people with disabilities, or other underrepresented groups not listed - and be unable to attend without some financial assistance. We have a strong preference for U.K. applicants to stretch our scholarship funds as far as possible.

Guidelines

Scholarships are awarded based on a combination of need and impact. Selection will be made by an assembled group of reviewers who will assess each applicant's request. Our target for PHP UK 2016 is to provide access to 50 or more Scholarship awardees.

If you are employed, and your employer is able to sponsor your travel, we will list them as a scholarship sponsor.

Scholarship recipients will be awarded an event ticket with the full benefits of a paid event registration, including lunch, snacks, and evening events. Each scholarship recipient will also be appointed a mentor that can help familiarize the awardee with the conference.

To apply, please fill out the scholarship or mentorship application. All application info will be kept confidential. If you have any questions, please contact diversity@vitisevents.com
